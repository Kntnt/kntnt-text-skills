# The city's new cycle network opens in August

*After three years of work, the connected cycle network is finally being inaugurated — 47 kilometres of segregated lane from the centre to the suburbs.*

By Anna Lindqvist

The council presented the final details of the cycle network, which opens on 4 July 2026. The investment has cost £234,567,000 and covers 47 km of segregated lane.

'This is the largest investment in cycling infrastructure in the city's history,' said Mark Eriksson, the project lead at the traffic department. He pointed out that the team consulted the Department for Transport (DfT) early.

The traffic department expects a 14% increase in cycling during the first year. Measurements are taken every other week between 14.30 and 16.30.
