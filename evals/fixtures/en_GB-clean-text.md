The city library reopens on Saturday 14 June 2026 at 10.30 after the refurbishment. The investment cost £1,234,567 and added 2,500 metres of shelving.

The proportion of visitors under 18 is expected to rise by 14% during the first year. Measurements are taken twice a week and the team plans to analyse the data quarterly.

Specialists from the Department for Transport were consulted on cycle-parking layout, and the council's accessibility officer reviewed every floor. The reading room is kept at 21 °C all year to protect the collection.

'We want everyone to feel welcome,' said the head librarian, Anna Andersson. The opening will be celebrated with readings, music and short workshops for children.
