The city library reopens on Saturday, June 14, 2026, at 10:30 a.m. after the renovation. The investment cost $1,234,567 and added 2,500 feet of shelving.

The proportion of visitors under 18 is expected to rise by 14% during the first year. Measurements are taken twice a week, and the team plans to analyze the data quarterly.

Specialists from the Department of Transportation were consulted on bike-parking layout, and the council's accessibility officer reviewed every floor. The reading room is kept at 70 °F year-round to protect the collection.

"We want everyone to feel welcome," said the head librarian, Anna Andersson. The opening will be celebrated with readings, music, and short workshops for children.
